# About CAT

* **Exam**: Common Admission Test
* Conducted by: Indian Institutes of Management
* Purpose: Admission into MBA/PGDM programs in top business schools

---

# CAT Preparation Strategy 

## 1. Core Strategy

One doesn't need multiple resources per topic. It's advisable to follow:

* 1 Concept Source
* 1 Practice Source
* 1 Mock Source

Repeat these consistently (Remember, consistency is the key).

---

## 2. Quantitative Aptitude (QA)

### YouTube (Free)

* Rodha — structured and comprehensive QA coverage
* 2IIM CAT Preparation — shortcuts and conceptual clarity
* Unacademy CAT — topic-wise playlists

### Books

* *Quantitative Aptitude for CAT* by Arun Sharma

  * Focus on Level 1 → Level 2
* *Quantum CAT* by Sarvesh Verma

  * Use for higher-level practice

### Notes

* Do not attempt to complete entire books
* Focus on selected high-quality questions and PYQs

---

## 3. VARC (Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension)

### YouTube

* Gejo Speaks — RC strategy and approach
* Unacademy VARC

### Reading Practice (Daily)

* Aeon
* The Guardian
* The Hindu

Reading is critical. CAT VARC is primarily a reading-based section.

### Book

* *Word Power Made Easy* by Norman Lewis

  * Use only for basic vocabulary building

---

## 4. DILR (Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning)

### YouTube (Primary Source)

* Elites Grid — high-quality DILR sets

### Practice Sources

* Previous Year Questions (mandatory)
* Anastasis Academy (YouTube + practice sets)

### Strategy

* Solve 2–3 sets daily
* Focus on set selection, not just solving

---

## 5. Mock Tests

### Recommended Test Series (Choose One)

* TIME
* IMS
* Career Launcher

### Budget Strategy

* Start with free mocks (Cracku, Unacademy)
* Later invest in one high-quality test series

---

## 6. Previous Year Questions (PYQs)

* Solve last 25 years of CAT papers

### Platforms

* Cracku
* 2IIM

PYQs are the closest representation of actual CAT difficulty and pattern.

---

## 7. 1-Year Study Plan

### Months 1–4 (Foundation Phase)

* Build QA fundamentals
* Start daily reading for VARC
* Begin DILR gradually
* Minimal or no mocks

### Months 5–8 (Strength Building)

* Start sectional tests
* Increase DILR practice
* Solve PYQs topic-wise

### Months 9–12 (Final Phase)

* Take 2–3 mocks per week
* Focus heavily on mock analysis
* Identify and fix weak areas

---

## 8. Key Principles

* Coaching is not necessary
* Consistency matters more than intensity
* 2–3 hours daily is sufficient if done seriously

---

## 9. Minimal Resource Stack

If following a minimal setup:

* QA: Rodha + Arun Sharma
* VARC: Gejo Speaks + daily reading
* DILR: Elites Grid
* Practice: PYQs
* Mocks: One test series

This is indeed sufficient for top-tier preparation.
